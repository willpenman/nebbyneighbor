Issue #4: Pre-Placed Neighbor Styling Variants

Three different approaches were explored for visually distinguishing pre-placed neighbors (given in the puzzle) from player-placed neighbors (placed by clicking). Each grid shows a sample puzzle with 2 pre-placed neighbors.

1. Minimal Theme Approach

Pre-placed (Dark Gray Solid)
Player-placed (Blue Solid)
prePlacedNeighborColor: '#2c3e50'
prePlacedNeighborStyle: 'solid'
neighborColor: '#3498db'
neighborStyle: 'circle'

Effect: Clean, professional look with color-based distinction

2. Bold Theme Approach

Pre-placed (Purple Outline)
Player-placed (Red Solid)
prePlacedNeighborColor: '#8e44ad'
prePlacedNeighborStyle: 'outline'
neighborColor: '#e74c3c'
neighborStyle: 'rounded-square'

Effect: High contrast, geometric styling with shape + fill distinction

3. Organic Theme Approach SELECTED

Pre-placed (Brown Filled-Outline)
Player-placed (Green Solid)
prePlacedNeighborColor: '#8B4513'
prePlacedNeighborStyle: 'filled-outline'
neighborColor: '#27ae60'
neighborStyle: 'circle'

Effect: Subtle, warm aesthetic with clear but non-intrusive distinction

Interactive Testing

Use the "Load Test Puzzle" buttons to see each styling approach with pre-placed neighbors. Click on empty grid cells to place player neighbors and observe the distinction. Pre-placed neighbors cannot be removed by clicking.

Selection Rationale

The Organic filled-outline approach was selected because:

  • Visual Hierarchy: Pre-placed neighbors appear foundational (earthy brown) while player neighbors are active (vibrant green)
  • Accessibility: The filled-outline style provides strong contrast without being harsh
  • Intuitive Understanding: Light fill suggests "given" or "foundation" while solid colors suggest "active choice"
  • Theme Consistency: Colors work harmoniously with the organic theme's warm brown grid lines
  • Extensibility: The color palette works well with constraint visualization added in Issue #6
